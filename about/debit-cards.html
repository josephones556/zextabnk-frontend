On January 29, 2020, we’re enhancing our Fraud Watch service to provide Zexta Bank Debit Card customers with more control over their transactions! Fraud Watch >

  What is Fraud Watch?

Fraud Watch is a transaction notification system that automatically alerts customers to potentially fraudulent activity on their Zexta Bank Debit Card. Potentially fraudulent activity is determined by the type, amount and location of the transaction.

  How does Fraud Watch work?

If a debit card transaction appears potentially fraudulent based on the type, amount, and location of the transaction, Zexta Bank may temporarily deactivate your debit card to protect your account. In addition, Zexta Bank will request a customer response by a verification text message or automated phone call, based on which contact information we have on file.

Upon receiving the verification text message or phone call, if the transaction is marked as authorized, the debit card will remain active/be reactivated and available for use. If the transaction is marked as unauthorized, the debit card will be deactivated/remain deactivated and will not be available for further use.

 

You may be prompted to reply to the Fraud Watch alert and rerun the transaction within 20 minutes, depending on the geographic location of the attempted transaction. If more than 20 minutes pass before you rerun the transaction, it may be declined again and a new alert generated.

  Is there a fee to use Fraud Watch?
No. Fraud Watch is free service offered to all customers to add additional fraud protection to their account(s). Check with your wireless carrier for fees that may apply.
  Do I need to enroll in Fraud Watch?
No. All Zexta Bank customers will automatically be enrolled in Fraud Watch notifications.
  How will I be notified of potentially fraudulent activity?
Customers with a mobile phone number on file will receive a verification text message, allowing them to respond to the text to indicate the transaction as authorized or unauthorized. Customers without a mobile phone number on file will receive a transaction verification phone call, allowing them to use the keypad on their phone to indicate the transaction as authorized or unauthorized. All customers will receive email notifications, in addition to one of the above contact methods, provided we have an email address on file. Please note it’s strongly recommended that the customer replies on authorized or unauthorized activity by text message or phone call; processing of email replies may be delayed.
  Do I need a smartphone to receive Fraud Watch notifications?
No. Fraud Watch notifications can be received via a text message or a phone call.
  Fraud Watch closed my chip debit card because of fraudulent transactions. What do I do next?

Please visit the Banking Center nearest you to begin the dispute process and obtain a replacement debit card. We can print your new debit card in our office and it will be available for use before you leave the office.

If you aren’t nearby a Zexta Bank location, please contact our Debit Card Services Department at (800) 749-7113 to request a new debit card be mailed to you.

  Are responses to the Fraud Watch notifications required?

Failure to respond may result in deactivation of your debit card to ensure the protection of your account.

  I didn’t respond to the Fraud Watch notification in a timely manner. What now?

Failure to respond may result in deactivation of your debit card to ensure the protection of your account. If your debit card is deactivated because you failed to respond to the transaction verification, please respond to the text message you received or return the Fraud Watch automated call to mark the transaction as authorized or unauthorized.

  What if I incorrectly mark a transaction as unauthorized?

Please contact our Debit Card Services Department at (800) 749-7113.

  Is Fraud Watch available within the Zexta Bank Mobile App?
No. Fraud Watch notifications and verifications are provided by text message or automated phone call.

Fraud Watch text messages will originate from 473-34.

  Which phone number will Fraud Watch use to originate automated phone calls?

  How do I unsubscribe from Fraud Watch notifications?

Unsubscribing from Fraud Watch notification will not remove your Zexta Bank Debit Card from the Fraud Watch system. Your debit card may still be deactivated if potentially fraudulent activity is detected; however, you will not be notified and will need to contact our Debit Card Services Department at (800) 749-7113 to verify the transaction. To unsubscribe from all Fraud Watch notifications:

  • Text Messages: Reply STOP to the most recent Fraud Watch text message
  • Phone Calls: Contact our Debit Card Services Department at (800) 749-7113
  • Emails: Contact our Debit Card Services Department at (800) 749-7113

Chip Cards >

What is a chip card?

Chip cards are debit and credit cards that contain a microchip. This chip technology offers customers an added layer of security, providing enhanced protection against fraud.

How does chip technology protect my information?

Each time a chip card is used at a chip-enabled terminal, a unique transaction code is generated that cannot be replicated; making it more difficult for criminals to fraudulently duplicate the card.

Will chip technology eliminate fraud and debit card compromises?

While chip technology will provide additional security and help reduce the risk of fraud, it cannot completely eliminate fraud and card compromises from occurring. It is still important to keep your card number and PIN secure.

When will I receive a chip debit card? Do I need to request one?

There is no need to request a chip debit card. We are issuing chip debit cards to all customers in phases. We will send a communication to the email address on file when your card has been ordered.

How can I activate my chip debit card?

You may activate your chip debit card by completing any PIN-based transaction or at a Zexta Bank ATM. Your current debit card will remain active for 30 days after your chip debit card has been ordered. Please activate and begin using your new chip debit as soon as possible to prevent any issue.

What should I do with my old debit card when I activate my new chip debit card?

Once your chip debit card has been activated, your old debit card will deactivate automatically. As your debit card number will not change, please dispose of your old debit card securely.

Will my chip debit card number and PIN remain the same?

Yes. Your new chip debit card will have the same number and PIN as your non-chip debit card, but the expiration date and 3-digit security code will change.

What if I want to change the PIN on my new chip debit card?

You can change your PIN at any Zexta Bank ATM or Zexta Bank location.

Do I need to update my recurring payment information when I get my chip debit card?

Yes. While your debit card number and PIN will remain the same, please update your expiration date and 3-digit security code information to prevent any issue with future payments.

Will I have to pay any additional fees to use a chip debit card?

No. There are no additional fees.

How do I use a chip debit card to make a purchase?

Chip cards do everything the magnetic stripe on your current card does. To make a purchase, insert the chip side of the card into the terminal with the chip facing up. Leave your chip card in the terminal and follow the prompts on the screen. The terminal will prompt you to remove your chip card when the transaction is complete.

Why do I need to leave my chip debit card in the terminal?

In order to authorize the transaction, the chip must maintain contact with the terminal to generate the unique transaction code. If you remove your card too soon, your transaction will be cancelled.

What if a merchant doesn't accept chip debit cards? Can I still swipe my card to pay?

Yes. If a merchant is not chip-enabled, you may swipe the magnetic stripe on the back of your chip card. Please be aware that swiped transactions are not protected by the additional security of chip card technology.

Will my chip debit card change the way I make purchases online?

No. Your chip debit card can still be used to make purchases online. There are no additional requirements for these transactions.

Will my chip debit card still work at an ATM?

Yes. You can still use your chip card to get cash, check your balance and more. If the ATM is chip-enabled, be sure to insert your card with the chip facing up. Your chip card will be held in the ATM and you will be prompted to remove your card when the transaction is complete.

Are there situations where I could experience issues when using my chip debit card?

Not all U.S. merchants accept chip cards at this time. In these instances, swipe the magnetic stripe on the back of your chip card.

Also, when traveling outside of the U.S., some unattended terminals require a different PIN technology than you normally use in the U.S. In these situations, find an attended terminal to complete your transaction or use an alternative payment method, such as local currency.
